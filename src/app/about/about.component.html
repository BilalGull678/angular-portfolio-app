About me.

My name is Barbara Calderon and I enjoy creating software out of an idea.

I am a Software Developer from Brazil. Portuguese is my native tongue and I am fluent in English.

My interest in professional software started back in 2018 when I had to capture, clean and analyze thousands of data for my Master's Thesis on International Political Economy. Turns out, solving a problem through coding taught me a great deal about programming, logic and automation. Next year, I was back into college to study computers and also landed my first job in the tech industry.

Fast-forward to today, and so far I have had the privilege of working with fantastic people in the industry.

I have worked with several technologies and developed valuable soft skills, including leading transformative changes to enhance team dynamics and organizational culture in a tech company. My technical knowledge grew through hands-on collaboration with developers and diligent study of documentation. I gained experience in programming logic, refined software craftsmanship, contributed to developing websites and REST APIs, collaborated on building visual dashboards, scrapers, crawlers, and participated in retrieving and processing data from various sources.

Working closely with teams, I helped implement microservices architectures using Docker, Kubernetes, Rancher, and ArgoCD. I contributed to developing asynchronous systems with RabbitMQ and optimizing CI/CD pipelines using Azure DevOps and YAML configurations.

I also contributed to creating and managing PostgreSQL databases, stream processing, and data pipelines. My work included implementing secure authentication using OAuth2.0 and JWT, conducting User Acceptance Testing (UATs), performing manual testing, and creating user manuals using LaTeX. Additionally, I developed end-to-end tests with Cypress and assisted in monitoring applications with Prometheus, Grafana, and centralized logging solutions.

I have to tell you — I know you probably hear this a lot from other developers, but it's true: my passion for technology goes far beyond work — I am the kind of person who finds joy in debugging tricky issues and learning the intricacies of new frameworks just for fun. It's one of the reasons I pursued a Master's Degree — I was driven by sheer curiosity to turn the unknown into the known. I love diving into documentation, exploring innovative solutions, and sharing knowledge with my peers. Whether I'm setting up a personal project to experiment with the latest tech stack or collaborating on a challenging problem, I'm motivated by curiosity and the thrill of continuous learning and continuous sharing. This mindset fuels my technical growth and, I believe, helps me contribute to a positive, dynamic team culture.

So far, we've covered the professional side. I am continuously learning and adapting to new technologies and methodologies. My goal is to create efficient, scalable, and user-friendly software solutions that make a difference. Whether it's front-end development, back-end services, or full-stack applications, I am passionate about every aspect of software development.
