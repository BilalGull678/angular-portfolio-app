About me.

My name is Barbara Calderon and I enjoy creating software out of an idea.

My interest in professional software started back in 2018 when I had to capture, clean and analyze a bunch of data for my Master's Thesis on International Political Economy. Turns out, solving a problem through coding taught me a great deal about programming, logic and automation. Next year, I was back into college and getting my first job in the tech industry.

Fast-forward to today, and so far I have had the privilege of working with fantastic people in the industry.

I had the chance to code in several technologies and also grow important soft skills: I led transformative changes to improve team dynamics and organizational culture. I have acquired code knowledge while working with groups of developers and studying documentation. I gained experience in programming logic, enhanced software craftsmanship, developed websites, REST APIs, built visual dashboards, scrapers and crawlers, and retrieved and processed data from various sources.

Some other things about me include: I have a passion for documentaries, purpose-driven organizations, black coffee, engaging book stories, teamwork, science fiction and suspense movies (they're the best!), pesto sauce, Linux, and the community experience. When travelling, I usually document my whereabouts and experiences so I can revisit them later.

I am continuously learning and adapting to new technologies and methodologies. My goal is to create efficient, scalable, and user-friendly software solutions that make a difference. Whether it's front-end development, back-end services, or full-stack applications, I am passionate about every aspect of software development.
